# Provenance Guard — Planning & Spec

Provenance Guard is a content-attribution service. A creator submits text; the
system estimates whether it was written by a human or generated by AI, attaches a
**confidence-aware transparency label**, records the decision in a structured audit
log, and gives the creator a way to appeal. This document is the spec — it is
written **before** the implementation and is the reference that travels into
Milestones 3–5.

**Stack** (`requirements.txt`): Flask (HTTP), Flask-Limiter (rate limiting), Groq
(LLM-as-judge signal), python-dotenv (secrets).

---

## 1. Detection Signals

Single-signal detection is not acceptable. The two signals are drawn from
**different families** — one local/statistical, one large-model/semantic — so their
blind spots don't overlap.

### Signal 1 — Stylometric analyzer (local, deterministic)

- **Measures** structural fingerprints of the writing:
  - *Burstiness* — variance in sentence length (humans mix short & long; AI is even).
  - *Lexical diversity* — type-token ratio (unique words / total words).
  - *Repetition / templating* — repeated sentence openers and repeated bigrams.
  - *Transition-phrase density* — connectors AI over-uses ("moreover",
    "furthermore", "in conclusion", "it is important to note").
- **Output:** a **score in `[0,1]`** = `p_ai_style` (probability the text is AI),
  plus the raw feature values (for the audit log and explainability).
- **Why it separates human vs AI:** decoder LLMs optimize for the locally-probable
  next token → smooth, evenly-paced, low-variance prose. Human writing is burstier
  and more idiosyncratic. Measurable with no model.
- **Blind spot:** structural only, no meaning. Short texts give unstable stats;
  formulaic *human* writing (legal, academic, ESL) can look uniform and be
  mis-flagged (false-positive risk); lightly human-edited AI defeats it.

### Signal 2 — LLM-as-judge (Groq)

- **Measures** a large model's holistic, semantic judgment of whether the text reads
  as machine-generated.
- **Output:** a **score in `[0,1]`** = `p_ai_llm`, plus a one-line rationale string.
  (The prompt asks the model to return a calibrated probability + short reason as
  JSON; we parse the probability.)
- **Why it separates human vs AI:** the model has internalized patterns of AI
  phrasing, hedging, and "assistant voice" that simple counters miss, and it reasons
  about coherence and content, not just structure.
- **Blind spot:** not ground truth — biased against non-native English writers, can
  be confidently wrong, susceptible to paraphrasing, non-deterministic, depends on
  an external API (latency/availability).

### Combining them → one confidence score

- Weighted fusion of the AI-likelihood: `S = 0.25 · p_ai_style + 0.75 · p_ai_llm`.
  The LLM drives the magnitude because it is the stronger semantic judge; the
  stylometric signal is a lightweight corroborator. (Started at 0.4/0.6 in the M2
  spec; **tuned to 0.25/0.75 in M4** — see §2 and the M4 tuning note.)
- **Disagreement penalty:** `d = |p_ai_style − p_ai_llm|`. Large `d` means the two
  independent views conflict, so we *lower* confidence and bias toward `uncertain`.
  Stylometry's main job is this **veto**: when it strongly disagrees with the LLM
  (e.g. formal human writing the LLM wrongly flags), `d` is large and the result is
  forced to `uncertain`. The system is only confident when both signals agree.

## 2. Uncertainty Representation

- **The final `score S` is P(AI-generated) in `[0,1]`.** `confidence` is how sure we
  are of the *label we chose*, not the same number as S.
- **What confidence 0.6 means to the system:** a weak, low-conviction call. It sits
  near the boundary — the signals only mildly point one way, or they partly
  disagree. The label must communicate "leaning, not certain." By contrast 0.95
  means both signals strongly agreed. A 0.51 and a 0.95 therefore land in different
  bands and produce different label text (this is the meaningfulness test in M4).
- **Mapping raw outputs → calibrated score (unified formula):**
  1. Each signal already returns `[0,1]`.
  2. Fuse: `S = 0.25·p_ai_style + 0.75·p_ai_llm`.
  3. Measure disagreement: `d = |p_ai_style − p_ai_llm|`.
  4. **One confidence definition for every band:**
     - `distance  = |S − 0.5| · 2` — how far the fused score is from neutral (0 at
       S=0.5, 1 at the extremes).
     - `agreement = 1 − d` — how much the two signals agree (1 = identical, 0 =
       maximal conflict).
     - **`confidence = distance · agreement`**, clamped to `[0,1]`.

     Confidence is high **only** when the fused score is decisive *and* the two
     signals agree; either being weak drags it down. That is what makes the number
     reflect genuine uncertainty — a 0.51 (near neutral, or with disagreeing
     signals) and a 0.95 (decisive + agreeing) land in different bands and produce
     different label text (the meaningfulness test).
- **Thresholds (bands)** — label class is chosen from S and d; confidence always
  comes from the unified formula above:

  | Condition                       | Label class              |
  |---------------------------------|--------------------------|
  | `d ≥ 0.40` (signals disagree)   | **uncertain** (override) |
  | else `S ≥ 0.70`                 | **ai**                   |
  | else `S ≤ 0.30`                 | **human**                |
  | else `0.30 < S < 0.70`          | **uncertain**            |

  The disagreement override is checked first: even a decisive-looking S is labeled
  *uncertain* when the signals conflict (the false-positive safeguard in §3), and
  confidence there is naturally low because `agreement` is small.

## 3. Transparency Label Design (exact text)

Confidence is shown as a **percentage** and reinforced in plain language so a
non-technical reader understands what it means. `{pct}` = `round(confidence*100)`.

**High-confidence AI:**
> ⚠️ **Likely AI-generated.** Our analysis indicates this text was most likely
> produced by an AI system (confidence: **{pct}%**). Two independent checks agreed.
> If you created this and believe the result is wrong, you can appeal it.

**High-confidence human:**
> ✅ **Likely human-written.** Our analysis found no strong signs of AI generation
> in this text (confidence: **{pct}%**). Two independent checks agreed.

**Uncertain:**
> ❓ **Attribution uncertain.** Our two checks disagreed or were inconclusive, so we
> can't reliably say whether a person or an AI wrote this (confidence in a firm call:
> **{pct}%**). We've labeled it *uncertain* rather than guess. If you're the creator,
> you can appeal to have it reviewed.

The uncertain variant is deliberately the safe default: the design's failure mode is
"admit uncertainty," never "confidently accuse."

## 4. Appeals Workflow

- **Who can appeal:** the creator of the content (identified by `content_id`; an
  optional `creator_id` from submission ties it to a person). Any classification —
  including `uncertain` — can be appealed.
- **What they provide:** `content_id` and a free-text `reason` (their reasoning,
  e.g., "this is my original poem, here is my drafting process").
- **What the system does on receipt:**
  1. Look up the original decision by `content_id` (404 if unknown).
  2. Append an **appeal record** to the audit log, linked to the original decision
     (stores appeal_id, content_id, reason, timestamp).
  3. Update the content's **status → `under_review`**.
  4. Return `{ content_id, status: "under_review", appeal_id }`.
  - **No automated re-classification.**
- **What a human reviewer sees in the appeal queue (via `GET /log` filtered to
  appeals / a future review view):** the original text snippet, the original
  label_class + confidence, both signal scores and their rationale/features, the
  creator's stated reason, and the timestamps — everything needed to make a call
  without re-running detection.

## 5. Anticipated Edge Cases

1. **Minimalist / repetitive human poetry.** A spare poem with short lines, simple
   vocabulary, and deliberate repetition scores as low-burstiness, low-diversity —
   exactly what the stylometric heuristic reads as "AI-uniform." Signal 1 says AI,
   Signal 2 (reading it as genuine art) says human → they disagree → the
   disagreement penalty routes it to **uncertain** instead of a false accusation.
2. **Very short submissions (1–2 sentences).** The stylometric stats are
   statistically meaningless on tiny samples (variance/TTR are unstable), so Signal 1
   is essentially noise. Plan: enforce a minimum length at the endpoint and, below a
   soft threshold, down-weight Signal 1 / lean toward `uncertain`.
3. **Human-edited AI text (hybrid).** AI-generated then human-polished to vary
   sentence length defeats the stylometric signal and may partly fool the LLM. This
   is inherently a low-confidence region — the system should land in `uncertain`
   rather than pretend precision it doesn't have.

## Architecture

Narrative — **submission:** `POST /submit` passes through the rate limiter and
validation, then the detection pipeline runs both signals in parallel; the
confidence scorer fuses their scores (applying the disagreement penalty), the label
builder turns the class+confidence into reader-facing text, the decision is appended
to the audit log, and the JSON response is returned. **Appeal:** `POST /appeal` looks
up the original decision by `content_id`, appends a linked appeal record to the audit
log, flips the content status to `under_review`, and confirms — no re-classification.

### Flow 1 — Submission

```
                 raw text
   Creator ──────────────────▶ POST /submit
                                   │  (rate limiter: allow / 429)
                                   ▼
                             validate text
                                   │ raw text
                                   ▼
                        ┌───── Detection pipeline ─────┐
                        │                              │
             raw text   ▼                              ▼   raw text
                 ┌─────────────────┐          ┌──────────────────┐
                 │ Signal 1        │          │ Signal 2         │
                 │ Stylometric     │          │ LLM-as-judge     │
                 │ (local)         │          │ (Groq)           │
                 └────────┬────────┘          └────────┬─────────┘
                p_ai_style│  + features        p_ai_llm│ + rationale
                          └─────────┬────────────────┘
                                    ▼
                          ┌───────────────────┐
                          │ Confidence scorer │  fuses scores,
                          │ (fusion + penalty)│  disagreement ↓ confidence
                          └─────────┬─────────┘
                        label_class, confidence, score
                                    ▼
                          ┌───────────────────┐
                          │ Label builder     │
                          └─────────┬─────────┘
                            label_text
                                    ▼
                          ┌───────────────────┐
                          │ Audit log (append)│  scores+signals+label+time
                          └─────────┬─────────┘
                                    ▼
                        JSON response ──────────▶ Creator
             { content_id, label_class, confidence, score, label_text, signals }
```

### Flow 2 — Appeal

```
   Creator ──────────────────▶ POST /appeal
     { content_id, reason }        │
                                   ▼
                         look up original decision
                                   │  original record
                                   ▼
                          ┌───────────────────┐
                          │ Audit log (append)│  appeal record linked
                          │  + status update  │  to original; status →
                          └─────────┬─────────┘  "under_review"
                                    ▼
                        JSON response ──────────▶ Creator
                  { content_id, status: "under_review", appeal_id }
```

## API Surface (contract)

### `POST /submit`  (rate limited)
- **Request:** `{ "text": "<content>", "creator_id": "<optional>" }`
- **200:**
  ```json
  {
    "content_id": "c_ab12cd",
    "label_class": "ai | human | uncertain",
    "confidence": 0.87,
    "score": 0.87,
    "label_text": "…exact reader-facing string…",
    "signals": {
      "stylometric": { "p_ai": 0.83, "features": { "burstiness": 0.0 } },
      "llm":         { "p_ai": 0.91, "rationale": "…" }
    },
    "status": "classified"
  }
  ```
- **Errors:** `400` missing/too-short text; `429` rate limit exceeded.

### `POST /appeal`
- **Request:** `{ "content_id": "c_ab12cd", "reason": "<creator's reasoning>" }`
- **200:** `{ "content_id": "c_ab12cd", "status": "under_review", "appeal_id": "a_77" }`
- **Errors:** `400` missing fields; `404` unknown content_id.

### `GET /log`
- **200:** `{ "entries": [ { …decision & appeal records… } ] }`

### `GET /health`
- **200:** `{ "status": "ok" }`

### Rate limiting (to document fully in README)
Applied to `POST /submit`. Planned default: a modest per-IP limit (e.g.
`10/minute`) because each call triggers a paid, latency-bound Groq request — the
limit protects cost and the external API from abuse while staying generous for a
real creator submitting a handful of pieces. Final numbers + reasoning go in README.

  bursty human paragraph, an even AI-style paragraph, a one-liner) and confirm the

  hit `/submit` with curl and confirm the JSON shape matches the contract.

- **Spec provided:** §1 (Signal 2 + the combining rules) + §2 Uncertainty

  response parsing, safe fallback if the API errors) and a `score(p_style, p_llm) ->
  {label_class, confidence, score}` fusion function implementing the bands + penalty.
- **Verify:** feed clearly-AI, clearly-human, and mixed/edge samples; confirm scores
  **vary meaningfully** (0.51 vs 0.95 land in different bands), that agreement yields
  high confidence, and that a forced disagreement produces `uncertain`.

### M5 — Production layer (labels, appeals, log, rate limit)
- **Spec provided:** §3 Label variants (exact text) + §4 Appeals workflow + the
  diagram (Flow 2) + rate-limit note.
- **Ask it to generate:** `build_label(label_class, confidence) -> str` (the three
  variants), the `POST /appeal` endpoint + status update, the append-only audit log +
  `GET /log`, and Flask-Limiter config on `/submit`.
- **Verify:** exercise inputs that reach **all three** label variants; submit then
  appeal and confirm status flips to `under_review` and both the decision and the
  linked appeal appear in `GET /log`; hammer `/submit` past the limit and confirm
  `429`.

## Scoring note (implementation-ready)
Fusion `S = 0.25·p_ai_style + 0.75·p_ai_llm`; disagreement `d = |Δ|`, `d ≥ 0.4`
forces `uncertain`. Bands: `S≥0.70`→ai, `S≤0.30`→human, else uncertain.
Confidence `= |S−0.5|·2 · (1−d)`.

### M4 tuning note (why 0.25/0.75)
The M2 spec started at 0.4/0.6. Tested against the four calibration inputs, the
stylometric signal proved conservative — it rarely emits high AI-magnitudes even for
clearly-AI text (that text often has naturally varied sentence lengths). At 0.4/0.6,
clearly-AI text fused to S≈0.66 and mislabeled as *uncertain*. Reweighting to
**0.25/0.75** lets the more-reliable LLM drive the magnitude while stylometry's real
value — the **disagreement veto** — is preserved: formal human writing the LLM
wrongly scores 0.8 is still rescued to *uncertain* because stylometry (≈0.23)
disagrees (`d≈0.57 ≥ 0.4`). Verified across all four inputs plus the §3 poet
false-positive scenario; all land on the intuitively-correct label.

Degraded mode: if the Groq API is unavailable, the scorer falls back to stylometry
alone and caps confidence at 0.60 to reflect the reduced reliability of one signal.
